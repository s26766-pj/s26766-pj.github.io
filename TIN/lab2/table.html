No. Latin Name Abbreviation Description
1 Cirrus Ci Thin, fibrous clouds found at high altitudes. Typically associated with fair, stable weather and composed of ice crystals.
2 Cumulus Cu Fluffy, white clouds with distinct shapes. Often seen on sunny days but can develop into rain clouds, such as Cumulonimbus.
3 Stratus St Uniform and flat clouds forming a gray or white layer in the sky. Can bring light rain or drizzle.
4 Nimbostratus Ns Dense, uniform clouds that bring heavy rain or snow.
5 Cumulonimbus Cb Powerful thunderstorm clouds, often reaching immense heights. Associated with thunderstorms, rain, hail, lightning, and strong winds.
6 Altocumulus Ac Mid-level clouds that can take various forms. Often appear as white or gray patches or bands in the sky.
7 Altostratus As Mid-level clouds that typically cover the sky with a uniform layer, obscuring the sun. Can bring rain or snow.
8 Stratocumulus Sc Low, layered clouds that often form large, white patches or bands in the sky.
9 Cirrostratus Cs Thin, high-altitude clouds that cover the sky with a uniform layer. Often obscure the sun, creating a halo effect.
10 Cirrocumulus Cc Thin, high-altitude clouds that appear as small, white, fluffy patches or bands.
